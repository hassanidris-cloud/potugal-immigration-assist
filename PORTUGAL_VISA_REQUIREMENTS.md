## GENERAL REQUIREMENTS (All National Visas)

### Core Documents Required for ALL Visa Types:

1. **National Visa Application Form**
   - Filled completely and signed by applicant
   - For minors/incapacitated: signed by legal guardian
   - Available from Portuguese consulates

2. **Passport Photographs (2)**
   - Recent, passport-type, color photographs
   - Even quality, good identification
   - ICAO standards compliant

3. **Valid Passport**
   - Valid for 3+ months after estimated return date
   - Minimum 2 blank pages
   - Photocopy of biographical data page required

4. **Travel/Medical Insurance**
   - Coverage: minimum €30,000
   - Includes: urgent medical assistance, repatriation
   - Valid for entire duration of planned stay
   - May be waived for bilateral agreements (Brazil PB4, UK S1)

5. **Proof of Accommodation**
   - Rental agreement, property deed, or
   - Hotel reservation, or
   - Invitation letter from host

6. **Proof of Financial Means**
   - Bank statements (typically 3-6 months)
   - Proof of income or employment
   - Sponsorship documentation if applicable

7. **Criminal Record Certificate**
   - From country of nationality OR
   - From country of residence (if lived there 1+ years)
   - Not required for applicants under 16
   - **Must have Hague Apostille** or consular legalization

## SPECIFIC VISA TYPE REQUIREMENTS

### 1. SCHENGEN VISA (Category C - Short Stay)
**Purpose**: Tourism, business, family visits < 90 days

#### Additional Requirements:
- **Flight Reservation**: Round-trip booking (don't purchase until approved)
- **Purpose Documentation**: Business invitation, conference registration, tourism itinerary
- **Employment Letter**: From employer confirming leave approval (if employed)

**Total Documents**: 10 items (7 required + 3 conditional)

---

### 2. TEMPORARY STAY VISA (Short-Term National)
**Purpose**: Seasonal work, study, research (up to 1 year)

#### Additional Requirements:
- **Work Authorization**: Employment contract or IEFP approval (if working)
- **Research/Study Documentation**: Acceptance letter from institution (if applicable)
- Longer-term accommodation proof
- Extended financial proof for 1-year period

**Total Documents**: 9 items

---

### 3. D7 VISA (Passive Income / Retirement)
**Purpose**: Living off retirement, investments, or passive income

#### Specific Requirements:
- **Proof of Passive Income**:
  - Pension statements
  - Investment income documentation
  - Rental property income
  - Social security benefits
  - Annuity statements
  - **Minimum**: €760/month (single) or €1,140/month (couple)
  
- **Bank Statements**: Last 6 months showing stable finances
- **Portuguese Tax Number (NIF)**: Required before application
- **Proof of Income Source**: Documentation of income origin

#### Income Requirements:
- Single applicant: €9,120/year minimum
- Couple: €13,680/year minimum
- Each additional family member: +50% of base amount

**Total Documents**: 10 items (9 required + 1 optional)

---

### 4. D1 VISA (Subordinate Work / Employment)
**Purpose**: Working as employee in Portugal

#### Specific Requirements:
- **Employment Contract**: Signed contract or formal job offer from Portuguese employer
- **IEFP Authorization**: Pre-approval from Institute for Employment and Vocational Training
- **Professional Qualifications**:
  - Academic diplomas
  - Professional certificates
  - If regulated profession: Portuguese professional order certificate
- **Financial Means**: Bank statements or employer's term of responsibility

#### Process:
1. Employer submits work authorization to IEFP
2. IEFP approves (if labor market conditions met)
3. Applicant applies for D1 visa with approval

**Total Documents**: 10 items (9 required + 1 optional)

---

### 5. D2 VISA (Entrepreneur / Independent Work / Business)
**Purpose**: Starting business, freelancing, or self-employment

#### Specific Requirements:
- **Detailed Business Plan**:
  - Executive summary
  - Market analysis
  - Financial projections (3-5 years)
  - Business model description
  - Target market and competitors
  - Job creation potential
  
- **Proof of Investment Capital**:
  - Bank statements showing funds
  - Proof of capital transfer to Portugal
  - Financial institution letter

- **Company Registration**: Portuguese company incorporation documents
- **Portuguese Tax Number (NIF)**: Required for business operations
- **Professional Experience**: CV, references, relevant expertise

#### Investment Requirements:
- Minimum capital varies by business type
- Must demonstrate economic viability
- Job creation potential is favorable

**Total Documents**: 11 items (10 required + 1 optional)

---

### 6. D3 VISA (Highly Qualified Activity)
**Purpose**: Teaching, research, cultural work, highly skilled employment

#### Specific Requirements:
- **Employment Contract**: For highly qualified position
- **Academic Qualifications**:
  - University degrees (Master's/PhD preferred)
  - Professional certifications
  - Official translations if not in Portuguese/English

- **Professional Recognition**: Certificate from Portuguese regulatory body (if profession is regulated)
- **Salary Proof**: Must meet minimum for highly qualified workers

#### Qualifying Activities:
- University professors
- Research scientists
- Cultural professionals
- Highly specialized technical roles
- Executive positions

**Total Documents**: 10 items (9 required + 1 optional)

---

### 7. D4 VISA (Student / Higher Education)
**Purpose**: Studying at Portuguese university or institution

#### Specific Requirements:
- **University Acceptance Letter**: Official enrollment confirmation
- **Proof of Tuition Payment**:
  - Payment receipt OR
  - Scholarship award letter

- **Academic Records**: Previous diplomas, transcripts, certificates
- **Financial Means**:
  - Bank statements (student or parents)
  - Scholarship documentation
  - Parental financial guarantee

- **Parental Authorization** (if minor):
  - Travel authorization from both parents
  - Copy of parents' IDs
  - Proof of guardianship

#### Financial Requirements:
- Tuition costs + living expenses
- Typically €600-800/month for living expenses

**Total Documents**: 11 items (10 required + 1 conditional)

---

### 8. D6 VISA (Family Reunification)
**Purpose**: Joining family member who is legal resident in Portugal

#### Specific Requirements:
- **Proof of Family Relationship**:
  - Marriage certificate
  - Birth certificate
  - Partnership registration
  - Official documents with apostille

- **Sponsor's Residence Permit**: Copy of valid Portuguese residence card
- **Sponsor's Income**: Proof sponsor can support family member
- **Accommodation Proof**: Adequate housing for family size
- **Family Reunification Declaration**: Formal request from sponsor

#### Eligible Family Members:
- Spouse or registered partner
- Minor children or dependent children
- Parents (if dependent on sponsor)

**Total Documents**: 10 items (9 required + 1 optional)

---

### 9. D7 DIGITAL NOMAD / D8 VISA (Remote Work)
**Purpose**: Working remotely for foreign employers while living in Portugal

#### Specific Requirements:
- **Employment Contract**: Remote work contract with foreign employer OR
- **Service Provider Agreement**: Freelance contracts with foreign clients

- **Proof of Income** (Last 3 Months):
  - Bank statements showing regular payments
  - Payment receipts from clients/employer
  - **Minimum**: Typically 4x Portuguese minimum wage (~€3,040/month)

- **Proof of Remote Work Activity**:
  - Portfolio of work
  - Client testimonials
  - Company registration as freelancer
  - Proof of ability to work remotely

- **Portuguese Tax Number (NIF)**: For tax compliance

#### Income Requirements:
- Solo applicant: €3,040/month minimum
- With family: Additional €1,520/month per dependent

**Total Documents**: 10 items (9 required + 1 optional)

---

### 10. GOLDEN VISA (Investment-Based Residence)
**Purpose**: Residence through qualified investment in Portugal

#### Specific Requirements:
- **Proof of Investment**:
  - Real estate purchase deed (€500k+ in non-urban areas)
  - Capital transfer certificate (€500k+ to investment fund)
  - Business investment agreement (€500k+ with 5 jobs)
  - Research/cultural donation receipt (€250k+)

- **Source of Funds Declaration**:
  - Detailed explanation of fund origin
  - Employment income documentation
  - Business profit statements
  - Inheritance documents
  - Property sale records
  - Investment returns proof

- **Bank Statements**: Last 6 months showing financial capacity
- **Portuguese Tax Number (NIF)**: Required for investment
- **Investment Documentation**: Varies by investment route

#### Investment Routes (2024):
1. Real estate: €500,000+ (non-urban/renovation)
2. Capital transfer: €500,000+ to Portuguese fund
3. Business: €500,000+ investment + 5 jobs
4. Research: €500,000+ to scientific research
5. Culture: €250,000+ to arts/heritage

**Total Documents**: 11 items (10 required + 1 optional)

---

### 11. JOB SEEKER VISA
**Purpose**: Searching for employment in Portugal (3-6 months)

#### Specific Requirements:
- **IEFP Registration**: Proof of registration on IEFP website as job seeker
- **Proof of Financial Means**:
  - 3-6 months of living expenses
  - No work permitted during search period
  - Minimum €600-800/month

- **Academic/Professional Qualifications**:
  - University degrees
  - Professional certificates
  - Relevant to Portuguese job market

- **CV and Motivation Letter**:
  - Updated resume
  - Job search plan
  - Target positions in Portugal

#### Requirements:
- Cannot work until job found and status changed
- Must have funds for entire search period
- Must leave or convert visa if job not found

**Total Documents**: 10 items (9 required + 1 optional)

## DOCUMENT PROCESSING NOTES

### Apostille Requirements:
- **Required for**: Criminal records, birth certificates, marriage certificates
- **Process**: Through competent authority in issuing country
- **Alternative**: Consular legalization if country not part of Hague Convention

### Translation Requirements:
- Documents not in Portuguese require official translation
- Translator must be certified in Portugal or origin country
- Some consulates accept English documents without translation

### Document Validity:
- **Criminal Record**: Valid for 3-6 months from issue date
- **Bank Statements**: Must be recent (within 3 months)
- **Medical Insurance**: Must cover entire visa period
- **Passport**: Must be valid 3+ months beyond stay

## AIMA / SEF PROCESSING

### After Visa Approval:
1. Enter Portugal within visa validity (typically 4 months)
2. Register with AIMA (formerly SEF) within 90 days
3. Provide biometric data
4. Receive residence permit card (temporary or permanent)

### Entry Declaration:
- Required if entering through non-controlled border
- Must declare within 3 days
- Fine of €60-160 for non-compliance
